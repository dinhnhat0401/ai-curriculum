## The Best AI Products Solve Expensive, Repetitive Problems

Signs of a good AI opportunity:
- Manual data processing (people copying between systems)
- High-volume decisions (same type of decision, made hundreds of times)
- Expert knowledge bottleneck (only one person knows how to do it)
- Inconsistent quality (results vary by who does the work)

Signs of a BAD AI opportunity:
- Requires 100% accuracy (life-critical, legal compliance)
- Highly creative with no pattern (novel art, strategy)
- The problem is actually a people problem (politics, motivation)
- "We need our own ChatGPT" (solution looking for a problem)

## Running a Discovery Conversation

The goal is NOT to sell. The goal is to **understand the problem deeply**.

Key questions:
1. "Walk me through your current process for X"
2. "How many hours per week does your team spend on this?"
3. "What happens when it goes wrong? What does that cost?"
4. "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
5. "If this were solved tomorrow, what would change?"

Listen for: time waste, error rates, frustration, "we've always done it this way."

## From Discovery to Proposal

Always start with a fixed-scope, fixed-price pilot:
- 4-6 weeks
- Clear deliverables (working system + evaluation report)
- Price based on value delivered, not hours worked
